  <h2 class="uvIdeaTitle"><a href="/forums/304921-excel-for-windows-desktop-application/suggestions/9320109-wizard-for-very-simple-merging-joining-of-two-exce">Wizard for very simple merging/joining of two excel files</a></h2>
    <div class="uvIdeaDescription uvIdeaDescription-truncated">
      <div class="full-text" style="display: none;"><div class="typeset"><p>Assume two excel files:</p>

<p>file 1:
<br />sku | product-title | picture1
<br />test1 | Sample product | <a href="http://www.example.org/foobar.jpg" rel="nofollow noreferrer">http://www.example.org/foobar.jpg</a>
<br />test2 | Other product | <a href="http://www.example.org/other.jpg" rel="nofollow noreferrer">http://www.example.org/other.jpg</a>
<br />test3 | Simple product | <a href="http://www.example.org/simple.jpg" rel="nofollow noreferrer">http://www.example.org/simple.jpg</a></p>

<p>file 2: 
<br />product-number | price_net | active | customer_group
<br />test4 | 12.34 | 1 | 12
<br />test2 | 13.45 | 1 | 12
<br />test3 | 17.95 | 0 | 12
<br />test3 | 16.95 | 1 | 15</p>

<p>In the wizard it should be possible to simply select the column &quot;sku&quot; for file 1 and map that to &quot;product-number&quot; of file 2.
<br />Excel should then generate a combined file with all rows where a &quot;sku&quot; and &quot;product-number&quot; matching exists:</p>
